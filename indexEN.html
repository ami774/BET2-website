  • 6.5% ≤ HbA1c ≤ 9.0%
  • Willing to undergo blood tests, wear designated pedometer with mobile app tracking during the experiment

Meeting any of the following criteria are not eligible to join the study:

  • Planned surgery in the next 6 months
  • Biological impairment or health condition affecting the ability to walk, such as blindness, physical immobility, and paralysis
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • On insulin for diabetes control

Purpose

Designed by pngall.com

Chronic non-communicable diseases such as diabetes are a pressing issue for society. More than one in ten adults in Hong Kong and mainland China have type 2 diabetes, with another 35% (388 million) in China classified as having pre-diabetes. According to clinical best practice guidelines, the first management procedure for a person newly diagnosed with type 2 diabetes is an intensive lifestyle intervention programme of healthy diet, physical activity and smoking cessation. However, these programmes are often designed with standard economic models of behaviour which relies on the assumption of rational decision-making. It can be difficult for those that have developed chronic health conditions linked to their lifestyle.

Techniques based on behavioural economics have been used to improve adherence to chronic disease management but have not been tested at the early stages of disease, where there lies an opportunity to delay or prevent the aggravation of type 2 diabetes for newly diagnosed people undergoing the lifestyle modification programme.

Content

A recent review of some small trials of behavioural economic-based incentives are effective in promoting health behaviours such as weight loss, physical activity, and medication adherence. To widen the scope of previous studies, we would like to further investigate the effects of behavioural economics-based incentives specifically on glycaemic control and lifestyle in adults with newly diagnosed type 2 diabetes.

In view of this, the School of Public Health of the University of Hong Kong would like to invite around 300 participants who have been diagnosed with type 2 diabetes within 1 year to participate in a 9-month randomised controlled trial starting in June 2021. Our aim is to investigate the effect of behavioural economic-based incentives and social incentives against a control group receiving standard care on glycaemic control and lifestyle change, to compare and suggest ways to lower direct medical cost for treatment for diabetes in the public sector in Hong Kong. In order to show our great appreciation, free pedometer and blood tests will be provided. Each participant will also receive a gift coupon upon enrolment and completion of the study.

Registration:

Please contact your doctor for this trial.






Enrolment interview:

Written informed consent will be sought from each eligible adult who is willing to participate. Patient information sheet will be provided. Fill in a short questionnaire for background demographics and personal information.


Blood sample:

Free blood tests will be provided to all eligible participants.




Randomisation of participants:

Participants will be assigned in differnet groups. Follow the instructions from the investigators.



6-month follow-up:

Fill in a short questionnaire similar to the one in enrolment. FREE blood tests will be provided.




9-month follow-up:

Brief follow up assessment to monitor changes in health status. FREE blood tests will be provided.
